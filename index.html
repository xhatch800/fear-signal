#1 AI Investment Bubble Threat to Economy

Reach

57% of economists view tech valuation collapse as greatest 2026 risk; major tech companies investing $500B+ annually while consumer spending remains under $12B yearly.

The fear

Massive AI infrastructure investment could create an unsustainable economic bubble that bursts and triggers recession or financial crisis.

Why it's contested

Supporters note AI companies like Nvidia generate real profits and revenue unlike dot-com bubble; critics point to 95% of firms reporting zero ROI on GenAI despite massive spending and heavy debt financing.

The strongest cases

Why some find it legitimate

Investment vastly exceeds consumer revenue, financial engineering obscures systemic risks, and concentration matches dot-com peak valuations at 35% of S&P 500.

Why others find it exaggerated

Fed Chair Powell distinguishes AI from dot-com era citing real corporate revenues; sovereign AI demand globally provides cushion; established tech has diversified business models beyond AI.

Questions worth sitting with

  • Will massive capex translate to profitable returns or massive write-downs?
  • Can precarious private credit markets absorb AI-related defaults?
  • Is this transformative infrastructure or temporary overinvestment?

#2 Social Media Harm to Children Level

Reach

Multiple countries from Australia to Norway to US debating age restrictions; jury found Meta and YouTube liable March 2026 in landmark case; global scientific consensus disputes harm severity.

The fear

Unregulated social media platforms are harming youth mental health at population scale through addictive design and psychological damage.

Why it's contested

Experts disagree whether causation is proven versus correlation; some cite evidence of harm, others argue benefits coexist with harms and bans won't address underlying drivers.

The strongest cases

Why some find it legitimate

Hospital admissions for self-harm in girls ages 10-14 doubled 2010-2014; major depression increases correlate with social media adoption; platforms use acknowledged addictive design features.

Why others find it exaggerated

American Psychological Association finds social media can be both beneficial and harmful; 25,000-adolescent longitudinal study found no relationship between time spent and distress; drivers predate internet.

Questions worth sitting with

  • Does restricting access address harm or just hide it on darker platforms?
  • Are causation concerns overblown relative to child welfare precaution?
  • Can regulation of design features work better than age bans?

#3 Iran Military Threat and War Risk

Reach

Trump threatened to bomb Iran's civilian infrastructure in early April; ceasefire announced April 7 but remains fragile with contradictory signals from all sides.

The fear

Escalating US-Iran military conflict could expand to catastrophic regional war destabilizing global oil markets and creating humanitarian crisis.

Why it's contested

Trump administration claims military pressure forced Iranian concessions; critics argue threats target civilian infrastructure, violate war crimes law, and destabilize negotiations; Iran remains defiant.

The strongest cases

Why some find it legitimate

Iran is advancing nuclear program, threatening regional allies, and closing Strait of Hormuz; military pressure extracted ceasefire and reopened critical shipping route.

Why others find it exaggerated

Threats to destroy civilian power plants and water facilities constitute war crimes; ongoing blockade and threats undermine credibility of negotiations; ceasefire repeatedly near collapse.

Questions worth sitting with

  • Will ceasefire hold or collapse under renewed threats and Israeli operations in Lebanon?
  • Do military threats reduce or increase long-term Iranian nuclear ambitions?
  • What international consequences follow US targeting of civilian infrastructure?

#4 Gun Violence Threat Level Consensus

Reach

Data shows Q1 2026 had lowest shooting deaths in 12 years (3,103 deaths); 48 states down significantly since 2021-2023; yet Virginia passed 25 gun reforms and federal bill proposed.

The fear

Uncontrolled gun violence continues killing thousands annually and mass shootings remain endemic national security threat.

Why it's contested

Gun deaths declining but remain politically contested; supporters cite decreases as vindication of current policy, opponents call continued deaths unacceptable and demand stricter measures.

The strongest cases

Why some find it legitimate

3,100+ deaths annually despite declines; Virginia shooting data shows 132 incidents in 4 months; jury found social media companies liable as partial cause of youth harm.

Why others find it exaggerated

Largest decline in a dozen years suggests existing policies and enforcement working; most deaths are suicides not mass shootings; Second Amendment constitutional protection limits regulation.

Questions worth sitting with

  • Do declining numbers suggest problem solved or still unacceptable baseline?
  • Can gun regulations pass courts or violate Second Amendment interpretations?
  • What drives dramatic 2021-2023 spike and subsequent drop if not policy changes?

#5 China AI and Intellectual Property Threat

Reach

Trump administration accused China April 24 of stealing AI lab intellectual property on industrial scale; yet summit planned next month amid competition framing.

The fear

China's systematic theft of US artificial intelligence research could allow rapid advancement enabling military and economic dominance.

Why it's contested

Officials characterize theft as existential threat; Beijing views reverse-engineering and parallel development as normal competition; experts debate whether espionage or market dynamics matter more.

The strongest cases

Why some find it legitimate

Official accusations of industrial-scale IP theft; cyber agencies warn of sophisticated covert networks; national security requires protection of AI technology.

Why others find it exaggerated

DeepSeek's success showed China developing capable AI independently; US companies operate within China and collect similar data; theft claims often conflate legitimate and illegal activity.

Questions worth sitting with

  • How does Chinese AI development compare to US in actual capability versus political narrative?
  • Can IP protection balance innovation openness with security?
  • Is AI dominance determined by stolen research or fundamental investment and talent?
